Frequently asked questions

What is All Fours in Ashes?
A single-player, folk-horror card roguelike built on the real card game two-handed All Fours (also called Setback or Auction Pitch). You play an 1850s medium calling the colonial dead to the table and laying them to rest, hand by hand, with your own candle as the stake.
What platforms is it on?
Steam, for Windows, macOS, and Linux / Steam Deck. There is no mobile version.
Do I need to be online to play?
No. It is single-player and plays fully offline. When you are online, Steam Cloud syncs your save across the computers where you play it on Steam, but a connection is never required.
Does the game collect my data?
No analytics, no advertising, no tracking. Your save lives on your device and, on Steam, in your Steam Cloud. See the privacy policy.
Does it support controllers and the Steam Deck?
Yes. Every screen is fully playable with a gamepad, including the menus and settings, with on-screen button prompts and in-game rebinding. It plays on the Steam Deck.
Does my progress sync between computers?
Yes, through Steam Cloud, across the machines where you play it on Steam. Each platform keeps its own local save in addition.
I have never played All Fours. Can I learn it in the game?
Yes. There is a How to Play guide and a hands-on practice hand built in. They walk you through trump, tricks, and the four points (High, Low, Jack, and Game) step by step before you face the real dead.
The game is hard. Any tips?
It is a roguelike, so it is meant to be a climb. Reach for bids you can actually make (a made bid feeds your candle), draft charms that answer the curse in front of you, and remember that losing a descent keeps your progress: the dead you have already laid to rest stay at rest.
How do I reset my progress?
Progress is stored in the game's local save and synced to Steam Cloud. To wipe it, delete the local save file and clear or disable Steam Cloud for the title in your Steam settings. If you get stuck, email me and I will walk you through it.
Are there in-app purchases or ads?
None of either. It is a one-time purchase, and that is the whole game.
Is it scary? Any content warnings?
It is folk horror: the restless dead, a guttering candle, a bleak burying ground, and an emotional late-game story of grief, a parent, and a child. There is no gore or graphic violence.
How do I report a bug or request a feature?
Email adamskijow@gmail.com with what happened, your operating system and the game version, and a screenshot or your save file if you can. It helps a lot.

Still stuck?
