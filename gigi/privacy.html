Privacy policy

Last updated: May 26, 2026

Card Night At Gigi's ("the game," "we," "us") is developed and published by Joey Beans Games LLC. This policy explains what data the game collects and how it is used. The short version: the game collects no personal data from players.

Data we collect

None. Card Night At Gigi's does not collect, transmit, or store any personally identifiable information about its players. The game has no analytics SDK, no advertising SDK, no telemetry, and no third-party tracking.

Data stored on your device

The game stores the following data locally on your device only:

  • Your game statistics (games played, games won, hands, scores)
  • Your unlocked achievements
  • Your settings preferences (sound mute, AI difficulty, etc.)

This data lives in the game's private storage on your device. It is never transmitted to us or to any third party. Uninstalling the game removes this data from your device.

Steam Cloud (Steam version only)

If you play the Steam version of Card Night At Gigi's, the same statistics and achievements may be synchronized to your Steam Cloud account so they persist across devices where you play the Steam version. This synchronization is handled entirely by Valve's Steam platform; we do not see or store the contents of your Steam Cloud save. Steam Cloud is governed by Valve's privacy policy.

The iOS version (Apple App Store) and Android version (Google Play) do not use Steam Cloud or any cloud save service. All save data stays on the device.

Permissions

The game requests no special permissions beyond what is needed to display the game and read/write its own private save files. No microphone, camera, contacts, location, photos, or identifiers are accessed.

Children's privacy

The game does not target children under 13. We do not knowingly collect any data from anyone, including children.

Changes to this policy

If we ever change what data the game collects (for example, adding optional cloud save in a future release), we will update this policy and increment its "Last updated" date. Material changes that affect existing players will be announced in the game's release notes.
